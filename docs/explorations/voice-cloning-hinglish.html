Voice Cloning in Hinglish

Preserving your authentic cadence — the pauses, the breath, the Hinglish rhythm — is what makes a clone feel like you, not just a generic voice. A practical, open-source-first guide.

7-step workflow Open-source first Prosody-preserving Ethics built-in
"Quality > quantity" is the rule that runs through this entire guide. 10–20 minutes of clean, expressive Hinglish speech — recorded with intention — will outperform hours of flat, monotone reading every time.
01

Capture your golden audio

Goal: 10–20 minutes of clean, expressive Hinglish speech that captures your natural tone, pauses, and emotions.

✓ Do's
  • Quiet room (no echo)
  • Speak naturally, as if explaining to a friend
  • Include variety: happy, thoughtful, serious
  • Leave natural pauses, breaths
✗ Don'ts
  • Reading monotonously
  • Background music or noise
  • Clipping (volume too high)
# recommended: record with Audacity / phone voice memo
Format: WAV or FLAC, 44.1kHz, 16-bit
Sample script: "बेटा, कैसे हो? मैं तुम्हें हमेशा याद करता हूँ। [pause 0.8s] आज बहुत खास दिन है।"
✓ 10–20 min ✓ 44.1kHz ✓ Hinglish
02

Transcribe + mark prosody

Why: To teach the AI your unique rhythm — where you pause, which words you emphasise, when your voice softens.

💡Use WhisperX (or any accurate transcriber) to get timestamped text. Then add prosody markers manually.
MarkerMeaningExample
[pause=0.6]short pause (seconds)"बेटा, [pause=0.6] मुझे गर्व है।"
[emphasis]...[/emphasis]stressed word"तुम [emphasis]हमेशा[/emphasis] याद आओगे।"
[soft]...[/soft]gentle, intimate tone"[soft]मैं यहाँ हूँ बेटा।[/soft]"
[breath]audible inhale"[breath] और फिर मैंने सोचा…"
# Example annotated transcript (plain text)
[soft]बेटा, [pause=0.5] कैसे हो?[/soft] [pause=0.8] मैं तुम्हारी [emphasis]हँसी[/emphasis] सुनना चाहता हूँ। [breath] आज बहुत याद आए।

04

Inject your rhythm (fine-tuning)

For XTTS or OpenVoice, you can fine-tune on your marked-up data to lock in your unique prosody. Even 5 minutes of annotated audio makes a difference.

# XTTS fine-tuning example (simplified)
from TTS.tts.configs.xtts_config import XttsConfig
from TTS.tts.models.xtts import Xtts
model = Xtts.init_from_config(config)
model.train_with_prosody(audio_files="path/*.wav", transcriptions="prosody_marked.txt")
~100 steps 2–5 min on GPU LoRA possible
🧬For IndicVoiceChanger (based on SeedVC), you can fine-tune with as little as 1 utterance for speaker adaptation. Ideal for Hinglish.
05

Generate speech with your prosody

Now feed your annotated Hinglish script into the model.

XTTS v2 (Python)
model.synthesize(
  "बेटा, [pause=0.6] कैसे हो?",
  speaker_wav="myvoice.wav",
  language="hi"
)
OpenVoice (demo)
clone_voice(
  text="तुम [emphasis]हमेशा[/emphasis] याद आओगे।",
  reference="myvoice.wav",
  style="emotion: warm; pause: 0.8"
)

✓ The output should now preserve your natural breaks, breath, and emphasis.

06

Fine-tune with audio editing

Sometimes AI still rushes a pause or flattens a tone. Use a DAW (Audacity, free) to manually adjust:

  • Stretch silences: select a pause, Effect > Change Tempo (without pitch shift).
  • Add breath: copy a breath from your original sample and layer it.
  • Pitch envelope: slightly lower pitch at sentence ends for warmth.
💗This extra step transforms "good clone" into "uncanny valley? no, that's just Dad."
07

Deploy & preserve

Store your model and generated files. For family use:

  • Legacy vault: keep triggered messages (birthday wishes, festivals) with exact prosody.
  • Family streaming: use WebRTC or a simple media server to stream to family devices.
  • Encrypt checkpoints: your voice is personal data — protect it accordingly.
