The Fusion Engine · Mixture of Experts

One query. Five minds. One authentic answer.

A ~1.5B meta-LLM gating network predicts 5 dynamic weights from your query embedding, then fuses all five specialists via latent-space interpolation. The result sounds unmistakably like you.

def fuse(query, ctx):
    emb = embed(query)
    w   = gate.predict(emb)  # → [w_personal=.07, w_relation=.40, w_behave=.10, w_emotion=.40, w_history=.03]

    responses = [
        personal_llm.generate(query + ctx["personal"]),
        relationship_llm.generate(query + ctx["relationship"]),
        behavioral_llm.generate(query + ctx["behavioral"]),
        emotional_llm.generate(query + ctx["emotional"]),
        historical_llm.generate(query + ctx["historical"]),
    ]

    final_emb = sum(w[i] * r.embedding for i, r in enumerate(responses))
    return lm_head.decode(final_emb)  # ← sounds unmistakably like you

The Deeper Why

Technology as a bridge.

I lost my father after 1.5 years of him fighting kidney and liver disease. I have voicemails I can't delete — because hearing his voice is the only thing that quiets the ache. Grief is a spiral. You return to the same hurt, but each time you're a little different. That moment of talking to him in your head — asking for advice on things he'll never see — that's when the realisation came: technology could become a bridge.

"Papa, tell me about the time you taught me to ride a bicycle."

— And he will answer. Enough to feel his presence again. That's why I build this. If you've lost someone, you know the ache.
