01

Data Collection & Preparation

High-quality Hindi / Hinglish speech data is the foundation. Indian languages require special attention for unique phonetics and code-switching patterns.

  • Record minimum 3–5 hours of clean, studio-quality speech
  • Cover all Hindi phonemes not present in English datasets
  • Mix of formal Hindi and casual Hinglish conversations
  • Multiple emotional tones and speaking speeds
  • Professional microphone in a sound-treated environment
02

Model Training Pipeline

A specialized training pipeline designed for Indian languages, with built-in code-switching capabilities between Hindi and English.

  • Hindi-specific phoneme mapping for accurate reproduction
  • Code-switching detection algorithms for Hinglish
  • Prosody preservation for natural Hindi speech patterns
  • Accent and regional dialect adaptation
  • Real-time inference optimization for live applications
03

Testing & Deployment

Rigorous evaluation to ensure Hindi accuracy and real-time performance before production deployment.

  • Hindi speech similarity evaluation — target 98%+ accuracy
  • Hinglish code-switching accuracy testing
  • Regional accent validation and preservation
  • Real-time latency testing <100 ms for live use
  • Continuous fine-tuning from user feedback

Timeline

Training timeline & process.

Week 1–2 · Data Collection Phase

Record 5+ hours of Hindi/Hinglish speech in studio conditions. Create accurate transcripts with timestamps. Clean and normalize all audio files.

Week 3 · Preprocessing & Alignment

Audio normalization, silence removal, and Hindi phoneme alignment. Split dataset into training (80%), validation (15%), and testing (5%) sets.

Week 4–6 · Model Training

Base model training using transfer learning. Hindi-specific fine-tuning, code-switching adaptation. Regular validation and checkpointing.

Week 7 · Evaluation & Optimization

MOS testing with native Hindi speakers. Similarity scoring against original voice. Real-time performance optimization for production deployment.

⚠️
 Important

Technical considerations.

🔤
Hindi Phonetics Complexity

Indian languages contain unique phonemes not present in English training datasets, requiring specialized phonetic mapping and a dedicated phoneme JSON config.

🔁
Code-switching Handling

Hinglish requires sophisticated algorithms to detect and handle smooth transitions between Hindi and English mid-sentence without audio artifacts.

🎵
Prosody Preservation

Hindi speech patterns have different rhythm and intonation compared to English, requiring dedicated prosody modeling to avoid robotic-sounding output.

🖥️
Computational Requirements

Training high-quality voice models for Indian languages requires significant GPU resources, optimized data pipelines, and careful mixed-precision training.

💎 Pro Tip: Start with a smaller pilot dataset (1–2 hours) for initial testing before committing to full-scale data collection and training. Validate your phoneme mapping and code-switching logic early — fixing them after full training is expensive.
