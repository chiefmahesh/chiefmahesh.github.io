🌀 The Emotion Wheel
What Is It

A map of human feeling.

The Emotion Wheel organises emotions into concentric rings — core feelings at the centre, increasingly specific emotions further out. Developed over decades of psychological research, it gives you a precise vocabulary for experiences that often feel unspeakable.

😊 Happy 😠 Angry 😞 Sad 😲 Surprised 🤢 Disgusted 😨 Fearful
📜
The Root
Gloria Willcox, 1982

The original Feeling Wheel by Gloria Willcox gave people a shared language for inner experience — mapping the emotional terrain in a way that felt both complete and approachable.

🧬
The Science
Robert Plutchik's Evolutionary Theory

Plutchik's Wheel of Emotions maps emotional intensity, from mild to intense. His work grounds the wheel in evolutionary biology — emotions exist because they helped our ancestors survive.

🛠️
This Version
Geoffrey Roberts — I Feel Wheel

Our wheel is a variation of Pastor Geoffrey Roberts' I Feel — Emotional Word Wheel, geared towards people with limited emotional vocabulary. The focus is on labelling, not intensity.

Who & When

Built for anyone who wants to feel more clearly.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑Who needs it?

Anyone interested in improving the way they experience and communicate their emotions.

  • 🧠People developing Emotional Intelligence (EQ)
  • 🛋️Those working with a psychologist or therapist
  • 💬Anyone who struggles to verbalise feelings
  • 📈Leaders and coaches building self-awareness
⏰When to use it?

Most useful when you feel overwhelmed or find your emotions hard to verbalize.

  • 😰When feeling emotionally overwhelmed
  • 😶‍🌫️When emotions are hard to put into words
  • 🌅On waking — to start the day with clarity
  • 🌙Before bed — to process the day's feelings
  • 📈Regularly, when building emotional intelligence
If you're working with a therapist, follow their instructions — some recommend using the wheel when you wake up and before bed. If you're working on emotional intelligence independently, the more you track, the more beneficial the process becomes.
How To Use It

Three steps. One minute. Lasting insight.

The wheel works because it slows you down just enough to observe what's actually happening inside you.

1
🗺️

Scan the wheel. Go through the list of emotions the wheel provides. Start at the centre — the six core emotions — then move outward into the more specific labels until something feels true.

2
🤔

Check in honestly. Do a quick check-in to see if that feeling is present within you at that moment. Don't judge the emotion — just notice it. More than one emotion can be present at once.

3
🗣️

Name it — out loud if possible. Tap or point to the emotion, then repeat the label in your head. If possible, say it out loud. Research shows that naming an emotion reduces its intensity and activates the brain's regulatory circuits.

Why It Works

Three core benefits.

There are many benefits to using an emotion wheel, but these three are the foundations everything else builds on.

⚙️
Benefit 01
Activate Executive Function

Naming an emotion activates the prefrontal cortex — the brain's "control room" for reasoning, decision-making, and impulse regulation. You literally shift out of reactive mode.

📈
Benefit 02
Increase Emotional Intelligence

EQ grows through repeated, intentional awareness. The more precisely you can label your inner state, the better you become at understanding others' emotions too — the core of empathy.

📓
Benefit 03
Maintain a Feelings Record

Tracking emotions over time reveals patterns — recurring triggers, cycles, and emotional weather. That record becomes invaluable in therapy, coaching, or simply understanding yourself better.

⚡

Research by Dr. Matthew Lieberman (UCLA) found that labelling emotions — "affect labelling" — reduces amygdala activity and helps the prefrontal cortex regain control. Put simply: naming a feeling makes it less overwhelming.
